A Five Minute Ritual

To Save You Hours

Hello there, my name is David Bassett. If you're anything like me, you struggle at times to exercise self-control in media consumption. What starts as "I'm just going to check my notifications" can quickly turn into hours of mindless browsing. So, I made this website to share what I do to refocus myself when I find myself scrolling aimlessly. It's extremely simple, but don't let that deceive you! Just follow the instructions and you'll be back on track in just a few minutes!

Breathe

Just close your eyes and take a deep breath. Slowly relax your body as you continue breathing for at least a minute. Just slow down and give your mind a chance to reset and recalibrate itself so that you can make inteligent decisions.

Envision

Once your mind has cleared, try to envision yourself at the end of the day getting ready to sleep. How would you like to look back on the day? What would you like to have acomplished?

Write

Find a small piece of paper (a sticky note would be perfect) and write down one thing you'd like to acomplish today. Don't just think it, don't write down more than one. Just write down one thing.

Act!

Now get out there and just do that one thing. No complaining, no excuses, just action. You probably still won't want to do it at first, but that's okay, motivation tends to sneak up on you after you get started. And when you're done, keep up the momentum and do another thing! It's easier to keep momentum than to get it back after losing it.
